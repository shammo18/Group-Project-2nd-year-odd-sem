DiceThrows

Problem Link : https://community.topcoder.com/stat?c=problem_statement&pm=4450&rd=7217

Tags: Probability , DP


Solution:

Let ,
a[i] = Probability of getting sum ‘i’ for player A.
b[i] = Probability of getting sum ‘i’ for player B.

Here , i can be any valid sum according to the constraints. (1 – 20000). To calculate a[i] for all i , we can use DP. DP states should be,*steps (how many times person A need to throw that dice) and *sum (means I). b[i] can be calculated same way. So,
the the probability of winning A , $$= \displaystyle\sum\limits_{i=1}^{20000} (a[i] * \displaystyle\sum\limits_{i=1}^{i - 1}b[i])$$. Here , we can use cumulative sum technique to reduce time complexity.
